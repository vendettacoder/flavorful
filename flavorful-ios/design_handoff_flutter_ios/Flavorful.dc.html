Best Lentil Soup

Rich, comforting, and surprisingly easy. Curry powder and a hit of lemon make this lentil soup something you'll come back to all winter.
Time
55 min
Serves
−
4
+
Difficulty
Easy
Saved
3 weeks ago
Ingredients
14 · serves 4
✓
¼ cup olive oil
✓
1 onion — finely chopped
✓
2 carrots — peeled & diced
4 cloves garlic — pressed
2 tsp ground cumin
1 tsp curry powder
½ tsp dried thyme
1 can diced tomatoes — 28 oz, drained
1 cup brown lentils — picked over & rinsed
4 cups vegetable broth
2 cups water
1 cup kale — stems removed, leaves chopped
1 lemon — juiced, to taste
to taste salt & freshly ground black pepper
Method
8 steps · ~55 min
01
Warm the olive oil in a large Dutch oven over medium heat. Once the oil is shimmering, add the chopped onion and a pinch of salt. Cook, stirring often, until the onion has softened and is turning translucent — about 5 minutes.
02
Add the carrots and stir to combine. Continue cooking until the carrots are tender, stirring occasionally — about 6 to 8 minutes.
03
Add the garlic, cumin, curry powder and thyme. Cook until fragrant — about 30 seconds. Pour in the drained tomatoes and cook for a few more minutes, stirring often, until the tomatoes have broken down a bit.
04
Pour in the broth and water. Add the lentils and stir. Raise the heat and bring the mixture to a boil, then partially cover the pot and reduce the heat to maintain a gentle simmer. Cook for 25 to 30 minutes, or until the lentils are tender but still hold their shape.
05
Transfer 2 cups of the soup to a blender. Securely fasten the lid and blend until smooth, then pour the blended soup back into the pot. Optional — for a creamier texture.
06
Add the chopped kale and cook for another 5 minutes, or until the kale has wilted into the soup. Remove from heat.
07
Squeeze the juice of half a lemon into the soup and stir. Taste, and add more lemon juice, salt or pepper as needed.
08
Serve hot, with bread on the side for dipping. Leftovers keep in the fridge for up to 4 days.
Notes
→
Make it vegan. The recipe is already vegan as written — just be sure your broth is vegetable-based.
→
Make it gluten-free. This soup is naturally gluten-free. Serve with gluten-free bread or crackers.
→
Storage. Leftover soup keeps in the fridge, covered, for up to 4 days, or in the freezer for up to 3 months.
→
Lentil variations. French green lentils hold their shape better; red lentils break down for a smoother soup.
→
Recommended equipment. A 5- or 6-quart Dutch oven, or any heavy-bottomed pot.
→
Curry powder. Flavor varies by brand — start with 1 tsp and adjust to taste.
